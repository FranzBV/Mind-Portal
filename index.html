Schedule a Mentally Healthy Day

Ever since the pandemic began, our regular schedule of activities have come to abrupt. What was once our daily routine of commuting, hanging out with friends, attending class, and other activities seemed to have been gone as we’re pretty much in our homes 24/7. This may have been uncomfortable. Establishing routines should be done to regain a sense of control and organization. For students, online school work causes them to focus on their screens most of the time, rarely having time for themselves. These routines should include times for leisure, rest, meditation, exercise, and learning.

Here are some tips that may help in scheduling a mentally healthy day:

  • Schedule your wake and sleep time according to your natural rhythm to stabilize your body clock
  • Dedicate a time for being exercise or other physical activities
  • Create a clear distinction between work time and non-work time
  • Have a time for social interaction via text, video conferencing, and other means
  • Have a time for self-reflection, rest, and relaxation
  • Make time to unwind and do the things you love
  • Limit screen time with your devices
  • Eat meals at a regular time
  • Evaluate what tasks you could realistically accomplish
  • Avoid scheduling a lot of activities that may lead to burnout and overwork
